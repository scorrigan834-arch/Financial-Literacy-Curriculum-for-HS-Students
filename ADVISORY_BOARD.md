# Advisory Board

## Purpose

The Financial Literacy Curriculum Advisory Board exists to ensure the curriculum remains practical, relevant, engaging, and effective for students.

The board will provide guidance on curriculum design, classroom implementation, industry relevance, and student outcomes.

---

## Areas of Expertise

We are seeking advisors from the following groups:

### Education

- High school teachers
- Curriculum specialists
- School counselors
- Career counselors
- School administrators

### Financial Services

- Financial advisors
- CFP® professionals
- Wealth managers
- Banking professionals
- Financial literacy advocates

### Higher Education

- College admissions professionals
- Financial aid officers
- Student success specialists

### Industry & Workforce

- Business leaders
- Entrepreneurs
- Human resources professionals
- Hiring managers

### Student & Parent Perspectives

- Recent graduates
- Parents
- College students

---

## Responsibilities

Advisory board members may:

- Review curriculum content
- Suggest improvements
- Identify gaps in learning
- Participate in pilot programs
- Provide classroom feedback
- Support curriculum adoption efforts

---

## Current Status

The Advisory Board is currently being formed.

Interested individuals are encouraged to contribute ideas, feedback, and expertise as the project grows.
