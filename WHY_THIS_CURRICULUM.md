# Why This Curriculum Exists

## The Problem

Most students graduate high school without understanding the financial consequences of many of life's biggest decisions.

They may be asked to:

- Borrow money for college
- Choose between multiple career paths
- Sign a lease
- Use a credit card
- Purchase a vehicle
- Begin investing

Yet many students make these decisions without having the opportunity to explore the long-term financial impact beforehand.

Financial literacy is often taught as a collection of concepts, definitions, and formulas.

Students may learn:

- What a credit score is
- What compound interest is
- What a budget is
- What a stock is

While this knowledge is important, knowledge alone does not always lead to better decisions.

---

## Our Belief

Financial success is driven by decisions.

The choices people make regarding education, careers, debt, spending, saving, investing, and housing often have a greater impact on long-term financial outcomes than any individual financial concept.

Students should have the opportunity to explore the consequences of these decisions before they face them in real life.

---

## What Makes This Curriculum Different

Most financial literacy programs focus on teaching students about money.

This curriculum focuses on teaching students how to make financial decisions.

Instead of simply asking:

> What is a student loan?

We ask:

> Should you borrow $120,000 for this degree?

Instead of asking:

> What is compound interest?

We ask:

> How much wealth could you build by starting today instead of waiting ten years?

Instead of asking:

> What is a mortgage?

We ask:

> Should you rent or buy in this situation?

The goal is not simply understanding financial concepts.

The goal is making informed financial decisions.

---

## Our Approach

### Decision-Based Learning

Students evaluate real-world financial choices and consider the consequences of different paths.

### Simulations

Students experience realistic scenarios involving:

- College financing
- Budgeting
- Career decisions
- Housing choices
- Investing
- Debt management

### Long-Term Thinking

Students explore how decisions made at age 18 can affect outcomes at:

- Age 25
- Age 35
- Age 45
- Retirement

### Real-World Relevance

Lessons focus on decisions students are likely to encounter in their own lives.

---

## Our Mission

Help students understand the financial consequences of life's biggest decisions before they make them.

---

## Our Vision

Every student should graduate with the knowledge, confidence, and decision-making skills needed to build a financially successful future.

We believe financial literacy is not just about understanding money.

It is about understanding the consequences of decisions.
