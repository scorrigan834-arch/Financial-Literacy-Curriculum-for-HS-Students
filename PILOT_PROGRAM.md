# Pilot Program

## Purpose

The Financial Literacy Curriculum Pilot Program is designed to gather real-world feedback from educators and students.

The goal is to continuously improve the curriculum through classroom testing and practical application.

---

## Pilot Objectives

The pilot program will help determine:

* Which lessons are most engaging
* Which simulations create the strongest learning outcomes
* Areas where students struggle
* Areas where teachers need additional resources
* Overall effectiveness of the curriculum

---

## Who Can Participate

We are seeking:

* High school teachers
* School counselors
* Career readiness programs
* Charter schools
* Private schools
* Financial literacy organizations

---

## Pilot Structure

Participants may choose to test:

### Option 1: Single Lesson

Test one lesson and provide feedback.

### Option 2: Single Module

Teach an entire module and provide feedback.

### Option 3: Full Curriculum

Implement the complete curriculum and participate in ongoing review discussions.

---

## Feedback Requested

Educators are encouraged to provide feedback on:

* Student engagement
* Lesson pacing
* Clarity of content
* Simulation effectiveness
* Suggested improvements

---

## Success Metrics

The pilot program will evaluate:

* Student confidence with financial concepts
* Student decision-making skills
* Teacher satisfaction
* Curriculum usability

---

## Long-Term Goal

Create a practical, accessible financial literacy curriculum that helps students make informed financial decisions throughout their lives.
