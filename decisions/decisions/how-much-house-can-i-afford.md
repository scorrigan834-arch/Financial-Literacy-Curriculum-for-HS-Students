# How Much House Can I Afford?

## Just because a lender approves you doesn't mean you should spend that much.

Buying a home is one of the largest financial decisions most people will ever make.

The goal is not to determine the largest mortgage you can qualify for.

The goal is to determine a housing payment that supports the life you want to build.

---

## The Real Cost of Homeownership

Many buyers focus only on the mortgage payment.

A home may also include:

* Property taxes
* Homeowners insurance
* HOA fees
* Maintenance and repairs
* Utilities
* Furnishing costs

---

## Questions to Ask

### Can I still save money each month?

### Can I continue investing?

### Can I handle unexpected repairs?

### How would this payment feel during a difficult year?

### Will this home support my long-term goals?

---

## Warning Signs

⚠️ Spending so much on housing that other goals become impossible.

⚠️ Having little or no emergency savings after closing.

⚠️ Assuming future income increases will solve today's affordability concerns.

⚠️ Buying based on lender approval rather than personal comfort.

---

## Reality Check

The best house is not always the most expensive one you can afford.

The best house is the one that supports the life you want to build.

---

## Final Reflection

> Will this home create financial freedom or financial pressure?
