# Student Outcomes

## Purpose

The purpose of this curriculum is not simply to increase financial knowledge.

The purpose is to improve financial decision-making.

Students who complete the curriculum should be able to demonstrate the following outcomes.

---

# Outcome 1: Evaluate Financial Decisions

Students can identify and compare the financial consequences of different choices.

Examples:

- College vs. trade school
- Renting vs. buying
- Spending vs. investing

---

# Outcome 2: Create and Manage a Budget

Students can:

- Track income
- Track expenses
- Calculate cash flow
- Build a realistic budget

---

# Outcome 3: Understand Debt

Students can:

- Explain different types of debt
- Evaluate borrowing decisions
- Calculate repayment obligations
- Assess financial tradeoffs

---

# Outcome 4: Build Financial Resilience

Students can:

- Create emergency savings goals
- Prepare for unexpected expenses
- Reduce financial risk

---

# Outcome 5: Think Long Term

Students understand:

- Compound growth
- Opportunity cost
- Delayed gratification

Students can evaluate how today's decisions affect future outcomes.

---

# Outcome 6: Understand Career and Income Decisions

Students can:

- Compare career paths
- Evaluate compensation
- Understand benefits
- Assess education requirements

---

# Outcome 7: Invest for the Future

Students can:

- Explain investing fundamentals
- Evaluate risk and return
- Understand diversification
- Create a long-term investment strategy

---

# Outcome 8: Build Wealth

Students understand:

- Net worth
- Financial independence
- Long-term wealth creation

---

# Outcome 9: Model Their Financial Future

Students can build a realistic financial plan that incorporates:

- Education
- Career
- Housing
- Debt
- Saving
- Investing

---

# Ultimate Goal

Students leave high school with the ability to make informed financial decisions throughout their lives.
