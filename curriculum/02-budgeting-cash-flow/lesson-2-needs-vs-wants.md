# Lesson 2: Needs vs. Wants

## Objective

Students will learn how to distinguish between needs and wants and understand how spending decisions impact financial goals.

---

## Essential Question

> How do everyday spending decisions affect long-term financial success?

---

## Learning Objectives

Students will be able to:

* Differentiate between needs and wants
* Evaluate spending decisions
* Understand opportunity cost
* Connect spending habits to financial goals

---

## Warm-Up Activity

Imagine you receive $500 unexpectedly.

How would you spend it?

List your top three priorities and explain why.

---

## Key Concepts

### Needs

Things required for basic living and functioning.

Examples:

* Housing
* Food
* Transportation
* Basic healthcare

---

### Wants

Things that improve quality of life but are not essential.

Examples:

* Streaming services
* Designer clothing
* Gaming systems
* Luxury vehicles

---

### Opportunity Cost

Every dollar spent on one thing cannot be spent elsewhere.

Example:

Spending $100 on a new pair of shoes means that $100 cannot be saved or invested.

---

## Activity

Classify each item as a Need or Want:

| Item                 | Need or Want? |
| -------------------- | ------------- |
| Groceries            |               |
| Netflix Subscription |               |
| Cell Phone           |               |
| Designer Sneakers    |               |
| Rent                 |               |
| Concert Tickets      |               |
| Gas for Work         |               |

---

## Discussion

Can some items be both a need and a want?

Example:

* Basic transportation vs. luxury vehicle

Discuss.

---

## Reflection Questions

1. What purchases do you make most often that are wants?

2. How could reducing some wants help you reach future goals?

3. Why do people sometimes prioritize wants over needs?

---
