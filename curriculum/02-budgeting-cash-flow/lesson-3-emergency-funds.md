# Lesson 3: Emergency Funds & Financial Stability

## Objective

Students will understand the importance of emergency savings and how financial preparation reduces risk and stress.

---

## Essential Question

> How can a small savings fund prevent a financial crisis?

---

## Learning Objectives

Students will be able to:

* Define an emergency fund
* Explain why emergency savings matter
* Identify common financial emergencies
* Calculate emergency savings goals

---

## Warm-Up Activity

Imagine your car breaks down tomorrow.

The repair costs $1,200.

How would you pay for it?

Discuss possible options.

---

## Key Concepts

### What Is an Emergency Fund?

Money set aside specifically for unexpected expenses.

Examples:

* Medical bills
* Car repairs
* Job loss
* Home repairs

---

### Why Emergency Funds Matter

Without savings, people often rely on:

* Credit cards
* Loans
* Borrowing from others

Emergency funds create financial flexibility.

---

### Recommended Savings Goals

Starter Emergency Fund:

$500–$1,000

Long-Term Goal:

3–6 months of living expenses

---

### Risk vs. Preparedness

Unexpected expenses are not unusual.

Financial planning assumes emergencies will happen.
