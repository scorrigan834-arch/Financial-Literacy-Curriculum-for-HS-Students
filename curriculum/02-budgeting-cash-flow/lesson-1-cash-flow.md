# Lesson 1: Cash Flow — Money In vs. Money Out

## Objective

Students will understand the concept of cash flow and how managing money coming in and money going out impacts financial stability.

---

## Essential Question

> Why do some people struggle financially even when they earn a good income?

---

## Learning Objectives

Students will be able to:

* Define cash flow
* Identify sources of income
* Identify common expenses
* Calculate positive and negative cash flow
* Explain why cash flow matters

---

## Warm-Up Activity

Two people each earn $60,000 per year.

Person A spends $55,000 annually.

Person B spends $70,000 annually.

Which person is more financially stable?

Why?

---

## Key Concepts

### Income

Money coming in.

Examples:

* Wages
* Salary
* Side jobs
* Investment income

---

### Expenses

Money going out.

Examples:

* Housing
* Transportation
* Food
* Entertainment
* Debt payments

---

### Positive Cash Flow

Income is greater than expenses.

Formula:

Cash Flow = Income - Expenses

Positive cash flow creates opportunities to:

* Save
* Invest
* Build wealth

---

### Negative Cash Flow

Expenses exceed income.

Negative cash flow often leads to:

* Debt
* Financial stress
* Reduced financial flexibility

---

## Activity

Calculate the monthly cash flow for:

Student A

Income:

* Part-time job: $900/month

Expenses:

* Gas: $120
* Phone: $75
* Entertainment: $150
* Food: $100

Questions:

1. Total Expenses?
2. Monthly Cash Flow?
3. Positive or Negative Cash Flow?

---

## Reflection Questions

1. Why is cash flow more important than income alone?

2. What expenses are easiest to control?

3. What happens if negative cash flow continues for a long time?
