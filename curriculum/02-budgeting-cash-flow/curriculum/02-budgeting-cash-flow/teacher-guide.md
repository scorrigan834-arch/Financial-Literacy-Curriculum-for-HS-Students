# Module 2 Teacher Guide

## Module Overview

This module teaches students how to manage money through budgeting, spending decisions, emergency planning, and cash flow awareness.

Students learn that financial success is not determined by income alone, but by how effectively money is managed.

---

## Estimated Time

4–6 class periods

---

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this module, students should be able to:

* Calculate cash flow
* Create a simple budget
* Distinguish between needs and wants
* Explain the purpose of an emergency fund
* Recognize lifestyle inflation

---

## Lesson Sequence

### Lesson 1: Cash Flow

Focus:

* Income
* Expenses
* Positive vs. negative cash flow

Discussion:

> Why do some high-income earners still struggle financially?

---

### Lesson 2: Needs vs. Wants

Focus:

* Spending decisions
* Opportunity cost

Discussion:

> Can a want become a need?

---

### Lesson 3: Emergency Funds & Financial Stability

Focus:

* Unexpected expenses
* Risk management

Discussion:

> What financial emergencies could happen to a teenager or young adult?

---

### Lesson 4: Lifestyle Inflation

Focus:

* Spending increases
* Long-term consequences

Discussion:

> What would you do if your income doubled tomorrow?

---

## Simulation

### First Apartment Budget Challenge

Students receive:

* Salary
* Location
* Housing costs
* Transportation costs

Students must create a realistic monthly budget.

---

## Assessment Strategy

Evaluate:

* Participation
* Budget calculations
* Reflection quality
* Decision-making process

---

## Key Teacher Takeaway

Students should leave this module understanding:

> Every financial goal begins with positive cash flow.
