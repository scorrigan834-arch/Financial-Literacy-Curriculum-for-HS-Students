# Simulation: The Real Cost of College

## Objective

Students will evaluate the financial impact of different college choices and understand how student loan debt affects long-term financial outcomes.

---

## Scenario

You are 18 years old and deciding between three college options.

You must choose one path and calculate the financial consequences.

---

## College Options

### Option A: In-State Public University
- Tuition: $12,000/year
- Room & Board: $10,000/year
- Total Debt After 4 Years: $48,000

### Option B: Out-of-State Public University
- Tuition: $28,000/year
- Room & Board: $12,000/year
- Total Debt After 4 Years: $160,000

### Option C: Private University
- Tuition: $45,000/year
- Room & Board: $15,000/year
- Total Debt After 4 Years: $240,000

---

## Assumptions

- Loan interest rate: 5.5%
- Repayment term: 10 years
- Starting salary:
  - Option A: $55,000
  - Option B: $65,000
  - Option C: $75,000
- Tax rate: 22%

---

## Student Tasks

### 1. Calculate Monthly Loan Payments

Use a loan calculator or estimate:

- Monthly payment
- Total repayment amount
- Total interest paid

---

### 2. Calculate Take-Home Pay

Estimate:

- Monthly income after taxes
- Monthly budget after loan payments

---

### 3. Lifestyle Reflection

Answer:

- What lifestyle can you afford?
- How much flexibility do you have?
- Could you save or invest money?

---

## Critical Thinking Questions

- Does a higher salary justify more debt?
- How long does it take to recover financially from each option?
- What is the true cost of prestige?

---

## Extension Challenge

Compare:

> Trade school or apprenticeship path with no debt

vs

> College path with debt

Which leads to higher net worth after 10 years?

---

## Reflection

Write a short paragraph:

> “What would you choose if you had to decide today—and why?”
