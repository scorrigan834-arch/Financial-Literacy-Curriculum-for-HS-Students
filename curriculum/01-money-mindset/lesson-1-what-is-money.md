# Lesson 1: What Is Money, Really?

## Objective

Students will understand what money represents, why it has value, and how it functions in a modern economy.

---

## Essential Question

> Why does money have value if it is just paper or digital numbers?

---

## Warm-Up Discussion

Ask students:

- What is money?
- Where does it come from?
- Why do people trust it?
- Could we live without it?

---

## Key Concepts

### 1. Money is a tool, not a goal

Money is a system used to exchange value, not an end in itself.

### 2. Money represents trust

Modern money works because societies agree to trust:
- Governments
- Banks
- Financial systems

### 3. Money is tied to productivity

You earn money by:
- Solving problems
- Providing value
- Saving others time or effort

---

## Real-World Example

A doctor, teacher, and software engineer all earn money differently, but the common thread is:

> They are paid for value created for others.

---

## Activity: "Value Exchange Simulation"

Have students list:

- 5 things they spend money on
- Who provides those goods/services
- What problem is being solved

Then discuss:

> “What would happen if that value disappeared?”

---

## Reflection Questions

- Why do we trust digital money?
- What makes one job paid more than another?
- Is money created or earned?

---

## Exit Ticket

In 2–3 sentences:

> Explain what money actually represents in society.
