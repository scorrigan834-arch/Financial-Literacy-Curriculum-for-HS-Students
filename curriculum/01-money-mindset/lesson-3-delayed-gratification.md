# Lesson 3: Delayed Gratification & Behavioral Finance

## Objective

Students will understand how everyday financial decisions compound over time and how behavioral choices influence long-term financial outcomes.

---

## Essential Question

> Why do small decisions today have such a large impact on future wealth?

---

## Learning Objectives

Students will be able to:

* Define delayed gratification
* Explain behavioral finance
* Recognize common financial biases
* Understand how habits compound over time

---

## Warm-Up Activity

You have two options:

### Option A

Receive $100 today.

### Option B

Receive $120 one month from today.

Which would you choose and why?

Discuss with classmates.

---

## Key Concepts

### Delayed Gratification

Choosing a larger future reward instead of a smaller immediate reward.

Examples:

* Saving instead of spending
* Studying instead of procrastinating
* Investing instead of consuming

---

### Behavioral Finance

The study of how emotions and psychology influence financial decisions.

People are not always rational decision-makers.

---

### Common Financial Biases

#### Present Bias

Overvaluing immediate rewards.

#### Herd Mentality

Following what everyone else is doing.

#### Overconfidence

Believing you know more than you actually do.

#### Lifestyle Inflation

Increasing spending as income increases.

---

## Activity

Imagine receiving a $5,000 bonus.

Allocate the money among:

* Spending
* Saving
* Investing
* Giving

Explain your reasoning.

---

## Reflection Questions

* What is one purchase you've made that you later regretted?
* How can delayed gratification improve financial outcomes?
* Which bias do you think affects people most?

---

## Exit Ticket

In one paragraph:

Describe a situation where delaying gratification could improve your future financial situation.
