# Teacher Guide — Lesson 1: What Is Money?

## Lesson Overview

This lesson helps students understand what money represents and how it functions in modern society.

---

## Time Required

- 45–60 minutes

---

## Learning Objectives

Students will:
- Understand the concept of money as a medium of exchange
- Recognize that money represents trust and value
- Identify how value is created in the economy

---

## Materials Needed

- Whiteboard or slides
- Student worksheet (optional future add-on)
- Writing materials

---

## Lesson Flow

### 1. Warm-Up Discussion (10 minutes)

Ask:
- What is money?
- Why do we trust it?
- Can money exist without trust?

Encourage discussion — avoid giving answers too early.

---

### 2. Core Instruction (15–20 minutes)

Explain:
- Money is a system of trust
- Money represents value exchange
- Money is tied to productivity and problem-solving

Use real-world examples:
- Doctors
- Teachers
- Engineers
- Entrepreneurs

---

### 3. Activity: Value Exchange (15–20 minutes)

Students list:
- Things they spend money on
- Who provides them
- What problem is being solved

Discuss as a class:
> “What value is being created in each case?”

---

### 4. Reflection (5–10 minutes)

Ask students:
- Why do we trust digital money?
- What gives money its value?

---

## Assessment (Exit Ticket)

Students write:
> “Explain what money represents in society.”

Look for understanding of:
- Trust
- Value exchange
- Economic systems

---

## Extension Discussion (Optional)

- Could society function without money?
- What would replace it?
