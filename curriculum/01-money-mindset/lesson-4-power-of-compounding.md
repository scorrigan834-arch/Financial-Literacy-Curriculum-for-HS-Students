# Lesson 4: The Power of Compound Growth

## Objective

Students will understand how small, consistent actions compound into significant long-term outcomes.

---

## Essential Question

> Why is time often more important than the amount of money invested?

---

## Learning Objectives

Students will be able to:

* Define compounding
* Explain exponential growth
* Understand the value of starting early
* Compare long-term financial outcomes

---

## Warm-Up

Consider two people:

### Person A

Invests $200 per month starting at age 18.

### Person B

Invests $400 per month starting at age 30.

Who will have more money at age 65?

Make a prediction before calculating.

---

## Key Concepts

### Compounding

Compounding occurs when earnings generate additional earnings.

Interest earns interest.

Growth creates additional growth.

---

### Time Is the Multiplier

The earlier you start:

* Saving
* Investing
* Learning
* Building habits

The more powerful the results become.

---

### Financial Compounding

Examples:

* Retirement accounts
* Investments
* Savings accounts

---

### Behavioral Compounding

Examples:

* Study habits
* Exercise
* Reading
* Skill development

Small actions repeated consistently produce significant outcomes.

---

## Activity

Use a compound growth calculator.

Compare:

Scenario 1:

* Start at age 18
* $200/month

Scenario 2:

* Start at age 30
* $400/month

Discuss the results.

---

## Reflection Questions

* Why is starting early so powerful?
* What habits compound besides money?
* What is one habit you could start today that would improve your future?
