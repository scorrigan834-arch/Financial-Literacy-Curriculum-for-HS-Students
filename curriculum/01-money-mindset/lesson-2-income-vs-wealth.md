# Lesson 2: Income vs. Wealth

## Objective

Students will understand the difference between income, assets, liabilities, and net worth.

---

## Essential Question

> Why do some people earn a lot of money but never become wealthy?

---

## Learning Objectives

Students will be able to:

* Define income
* Define wealth
* Define assets
* Define liabilities
* Calculate simple net worth

---

## Warm-Up

Consider two individuals:

Person A:

* Earns $250,000 per year
* Owes $500,000 on various debts
* Has very little savings

Person B:

* Earns $75,000 per year
* Has no debt
* Has $250,000 invested

Who is wealthier?

Discuss as a class.

---

## Key Concepts

### Income

Money earned from:

* Employment
* Business ownership
* Investments

Income is a flow of money.

---

### Wealth

The value of everything you own minus everything you owe.

Wealth is a stock of value.

---

### Assets

Things that have value or generate future value.

Examples:

* Cash
* Investments
* Real estate
* Businesses

---

### Liabilities

Financial obligations.

Examples:

* Student loans
* Credit card debt
* Auto loans
* Mortgages

---

### Net Worth

Formula:

Net Worth = Assets − Liabilities

---

## Activity

Create a personal balance sheet using fictional numbers.

Calculate:

* Total Assets
* Total Liabilities
* Net Worth

---

## Reflection Questions

* Why can high-income earners still struggle financially?
* Why is net worth often a better measure than income?
* What habits contribute to long-term wealth building?

---

## Exit Ticket

In one paragraph:

Explain the difference between income and wealth.
