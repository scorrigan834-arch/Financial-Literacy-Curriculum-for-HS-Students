# The Case for Change

## The Problem

Millions of students graduate each year and quickly face major financial decisions.

These decisions often include:

- Student loans
- Credit cards
- Housing
- Transportation
- Career choices
- Investing

Many students make these decisions without understanding the long-term financial consequences.

---

## The Cost of Poor Financial Decisions

Poor financial decisions can lead to:

- Excessive student loan debt
- High-interest credit card debt
- Limited financial flexibility
- Delayed wealth building
- Increased financial stress

Many of these outcomes begin with decisions made between the ages of 17 and 25.

---

## The Gap

Traditional financial literacy programs often focus on concepts.

Students learn:

- Definitions
- Terminology
- Formulas

However, students are rarely asked to practice evaluating real-world financial decisions.

---

## What Students Need

Students need opportunities to:

- Compare alternatives
- Evaluate tradeoffs
- Model long-term consequences
- Understand opportunity costs
- Practice decision-making

before those decisions become real.

---

## Our Solution

This curriculum focuses on decision-based financial literacy.

Students learn through:

- Simulations
- Real-world scenarios
- Financial modeling
- Long-term consequence analysis

The goal is not simply to teach students about money.

The goal is to help students make better financial decisions.

---

## Our Vision

Help every student understand the financial consequences of life's biggest decisions before they make them.
