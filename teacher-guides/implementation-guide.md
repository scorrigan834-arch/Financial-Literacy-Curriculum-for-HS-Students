# Teacher Implementation Guide

## Overview

This curriculum is designed to help high school students understand real-world financial decision-making through lessons, discussions, and simulations.

It focuses on practical financial literacy, not theory.

---

## Recommended Course Structure

### Option 1: Semester Course (Recommended)

- 12–16 weeks
- 2–3 classes per week
- Includes all core modules + simulations

### Option 2: Year-Long Course

- Expanded projects and deeper simulations
- Additional investment and career modules
- Extended capstone project

---

## How to Teach This Curriculum

Each unit includes:

1. Lesson content (student-facing)
2. Teacher guide
3. Activity or simulation
4. Worksheet or assessment

Teachers should follow this flow:

> Introduce concept → Discuss → Apply through simulation → Reflect

---

## Teaching Philosophy

This curriculum is built on three principles:

### 1. Real-World Application
Students learn by making decisions, not memorizing definitions.

### 2. Behavioral Learning
We focus on how financial behavior impacts long-term outcomes.

### 3. Experiential Learning
Students experience financial consequences in simulations.

---

## Classroom Flow Example (1 Lesson)

1. Warm-up discussion (10 min)
2. Concept introduction (15 min)
3. Simulation activity (20–30 min)
4. Reflection (10 min)

---

## Assessment Approach

Students are evaluated on:

- Understanding of concepts
- Decision-making reasoning
- Ability to interpret financial tradeoffs
- Reflection quality (not just math accuracy)

---

## Required Tools

- Calculator (basic or spreadsheet)
- Printed worksheets OR digital version
- Optional: Google Sheets or laptop access

---

## Teacher Notes

- Students may initially make unrealistic financial assumptions
- Encourage discussion, not correction-only teaching
- Emphasize long-term thinking over “right answers”

---

## Capstone Project

Students complete:

> "Design Your Financial Future"

They choose:

- Education path
- Career path
- Living situation

Then calculate:

- Debt
- Income
- Savings
- Net worth trajectory

---

## Goal of This Curriculum

Students should leave with the ability to answer:

> “What are the financial consequences of my life decisions?”
